Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)


What is Autism Spectrum Disorder?

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a neurodevelopmental condition that affects how individuals perceive, communicate, and interact with the world around them. The term "spectrum" reflects the wide range of challenges and strengths individuals with ASD may experience.

Signs and Symptoms of ASD

  • Social Challenges: – Difficulty understanding social cues, maintaining eye contact, or forming relationships.
  • Communication Differences: –Delayed speech, echolalia (repeating phrases), or alternative forms of communication.
  • Repetitive Behaviors: – Routines, rituals, or repeated actions.
  • Intense Interests: – A deep focus on specific topics, hobbies, or objects.
  • Sensory Sensitivities: – Overreaction or underreaction to sounds, lights, textures, or other sensory inputs.
How We Support Individuals with ASD

We create customized therapy programs tailored to the unique strengths and needs of each individual. Our services include:

  • Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA): – Focused on developing positive behaviors and reducing challenges.
  • Speech and Language Therapy:– Enhances communication skills, including verbal and nonverbal methods.
  • Occupational Therapy: – Builds independence through daily living and sensory integration support.
  • Social Skills Training: – Helps individuals navigate social situations and relationships.

Parent and Family Support

    We offer workshops, counseling, and resources to help families understand and support their loved ones with ASD.
